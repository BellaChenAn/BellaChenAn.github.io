And... I'm a researcher.

I’m currently focused on research in HCI, especially on understanding the needs of vulnerable groups. My work looks at user behavior and how design and technology can help people live better lives. I’ve done research at Shandong University, Tsinghua University, and the University of Waterloo, mainly on topics like children, education, and healthcare. I am currently a member of the HCI+Health Lab and the Safe Interactions Lab at the University of Waterloo.

Development of BCI-Neurofeedback Cognitive Rehabilitation Games to Support Attention Training for Children with ADHD

Neurofeedback is a promising method for attention training in children with ADHD. In this work, we developed a neurofeedback game called AttentionGo, incorporating creativity to enhance motivation. After consulting instructors, parents, and testing with ADHD children, the game demonstrated great potential for future design improvements.

Learn more

Exploring the effects of different BCI-based attention training games on the brain: A functional near-infrared spectroscopy study

This study compares the effectiveness of video games (VG) and physical games (PG) in BCI-based attention training, using fNIRS to monitor brain activation. Results show PG activates more brain regions and intrinsic motivation than VG, with notable gender differences. These findings provide insights for designing more effective BCI-based rehabilitation games.

Learn more

Exploring the Design Space of a Technology-mediated Calm-down Corner

Preschool educators play a key role in helping children manage emotions, but it's challenging to track emotional changes and intervene for every child in a class. This study explores how technology can support educators using "calm-down corners" through interviews and co-design sessions with 22 preschool educators. The findings highlight challenges and provide design insights for future technology-enabled systems to aid emotion regulation.
